|  A BILL TO BE ENTITLED  
|  AN ACT  
| relating to the compilation and reporting of statistics involving  
| sexual assault victims who receive a forensic medical examination  
| before reporting the assault to law enforcement.  
|  BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF TEXAS:  
|  SECTION 1. Subchapter A, Chapter 56, Code of Criminal  
| Procedure, is amended by adding Article 56.0651 to read as follows:  
|  _Art._ ___56.0651._ _____DELAYED REPORTING BY SEXUAL ASSAULT VICTIMS;_  
| _COMPILATION OF STATISTICS. (a)_ ___In this article, "department"_  
| _means the Department of Public Safety._  
|  _(b)_ _____The department shall compile and maintain statistics on_  
| _the number of victims of sexual assault in each calendar year who_  
| _have reported the sexual assault to a law enforcement agency after_  
| _receiving a forensic medical examination under Article 56.065,_  
| _regardless of the year in which that examination was performed._ __  
| _Statistics compiled and maintained under this subsection may not_  
| _include identifying information of a victim._  
|  _(c)_ _____Not later than September 1 of each even-numbered year,_  
| _the department shall:_  
|  _(1)_ _____report the statistics compiled by the department_  
| _under Subsection (b) to the standing committees of the house of_  
| _representatives and senate with primary jurisdiction over criminal_  
| _justice issues; and_  
|  _(2)_ _____post the statistics compiled by the department_  
| _under Subsection (b) on the department's Internet website._  
|  SECTION 2. This Act takes effect September 1, 2019.
