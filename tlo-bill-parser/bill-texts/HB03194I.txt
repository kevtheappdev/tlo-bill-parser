|  A BILL TO BE ENTITLED  
|  AN ACT  
| relating to the regulation of the practice of acupuncture.  
|  BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF TEXAS:  
|  SECTION 1. Section 205.001, Occupations Code, is amended by  
| amending Subdivisions (2) and (4) and adding Subdivision (2-a) to  
| read as follows:  
|  (2) "Acupuncture" means:  
|  (A) the nonsurgical, _incisive_ [ ~~nonincisive~~ ]  
| insertion of an acupuncture needle and the application of  
| moxibustion to specific areas of the human body as a primary mode of  
| therapy to treat and mitigate a human condition, including  
| evaluation and assessment of the condition; and  
|  (B) _in conjunction with the treatment described_  
| _by Paragraph (A):_  
|  _(i)_ the administration of thermal or  
| electrical treatments [ ~~or the recommendation of dietary~~  
|  ~~guidelines~~ ], energy flow exercise, or dietary or herbal  
| supplements _; or_  
|  _(ii)_ _____the recommendation of dietary_  
| _guidelines_ [ ~~in conjunction with the treatment described by~~  
|  ~~Paragraph (A)~~ ].  
|  _(2-a)_ _____"Acupuncture needle" means a device described by_  
| _21 C.F.R. Section 880.5580, as that section existed on January 1,_  
| _2019._  
|  (4) "Acupuncturist" means a person who:  
|  (A) _holds a license to practice_ [ ~~practices~~ ]  
| acupuncture _issued under this chapter_ ; and  
|  (B) directly or indirectly charges a fee for the  
| performance of acupuncture services.  
|  SECTION 2. This Act takes effect September 1, 2019.
